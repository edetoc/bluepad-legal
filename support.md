## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I start a session?

Open bluepad and tap the large **START** button on the main screen. Your session begins immediately, with GPS, time, distance, and heart rate tracking. You can also start a session straight from the Apple Watch app.

### Why isn't my Apple Watch syncing with my iPhone?

bluepad uses iCloud to sync sessions between your iPhone and Apple Watch. Make sure:

- Both devices are signed in to the same Apple ID
- iCloud is enabled in **Settings → [Your Name] → iCloud**
- bluepad is installed on both devices
- Both devices have an internet connection

Sync usually happens within a few seconds. If a session doesn't appear, try opening bluepad on both devices once.

### Where are my workouts saved in the Health app?

Each session is saved as a **Paddle Sports** workout in the Health app on iPhone. Open Health → **Browse → Activity → Workouts** to see them, or check your **Summary → Trends**.

### How do I restore my purchase on a new device?

Open bluepad, go to the paywall, and tap **Restore Purchases**. As long as you're signed in to the same Apple ID you used to buy bluepad, your purchase will be restored automatically.

### Can I get a refund?

Refunds for App Store purchases are handled directly by Apple. Visit [reportaproblem.apple.com](https://reportaproblem.apple.com), sign in with your Apple ID, find your bluepad purchase, and request a refund.

### Does bluepad work without an internet connection?

Yes. GPS tracking, session recording, and heart rate work fully offline. Weather and iCloud sync require an internet connection.

### How do I delete my data?

To delete a single session, open it in your session history and tap the trash icon. To remove all bluepad data:

- Delete the app from your iPhone (and Apple Watch, if installed)
- Open **Settings → [Your Name] → iCloud → Manage Storage → bluepad** and remove the data from iCloud as well

bluepad stores all your data on your device and in your private iCloud account. Nothing is collected or stored on any external server.
